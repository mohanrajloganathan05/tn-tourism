Travel Tips & Information


Everything you need to know to make your journey through Tamil Nadu smooth and enjoyable.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Tamil Nadu is between October and March when the weather is cooler and more pleasant. Summer (April to June) can be extremely hot, especially in inland areas.

Getting Around

Tamil Nadu has excellent public transportation options including buses, trains, and auto-rickshaws. For remote areas, consider hiring a private car with a driver. Major cities like Chennai have metro rail services.

Local Cuisine

Tamil cuisine is known for its use of rice, lentils, and spices. Don't miss trying traditional dishes like Dosa, Idli, Sambar, Pongal, and the famous "meals" served on banana leaves. Street food is delicious but choose vendors with good hygiene practices.

Cultural Etiquette

When visiting temples, dress modestly with shoulders and knees covered. Remove shoes before entering. Taking photos inside temples may be restricted in some areas. It's customary to eat with your right hand if dining traditionally.

Festivals Worth Experiencing

Pongal (mid-January), Tamil New Year (April), Madurai Chithirai Festival (April-May), and Dance Festival at Mamallapuram (December-January) are major cultural celebrations that showcase Tamil traditions, music, and dance.

Shopping Guide

Tamil Nadu is famous for silk sarees (especially from Kanchipuram), bronze statues, Tanjore paintings, wood carvings, and handloom textiles. Government emporiums like Poompuhar and Co-optex offer authentic items at fixed prices.

Weather & Climate


Winter (Nov-Feb)

22°C - 30°C

Pleasant and mild temperatures, ideal for sightseeing and outdoor activities.

Summer (Mar-Jun)

30°C - 40°C

Hot and dry, hill stations like Yercaud, Ooty and Kodaikanal offer relief from the heat.

Monsoon (Jul-Sep)

25°C - 35°C

North-east monsoon brings moderate rainfall, creating lush greenery.

Post-Monsoon (Oct)

25°C - 32°C

Occasional heavy rains due to retreating north-east monsoon.

Essential Information

Languages

Tamil is the official language of Tamil Nadu. English is widely understood in urban areas and tourist destinations. Learning a few basic Tamil phrases can enhance your experience.

Hello: Vanakkam

Thank you: Nandri

How are you?: Eppadi irukeenga?

Currency & Payments

The Indian Rupee (₹) is the currency. ATMs are readily available in cities and tourist areas. Credit cards are accepted at major establishments, but carry cash for smaller vendors and rural areas.

Mobile payment options like Google Pay, PhonePe, and Paytm are widely used in urban centers.

Connectivity

Mobile coverage is generally good across Tamil Nadu. Major providers include Airtel, Jio, and Vi. Tourist SIM cards are available at airports with valid ID and passport-sized photos.

Most hotels, restaurants, and cafes offer free Wi-Fi, though speeds may vary in rural areas.
